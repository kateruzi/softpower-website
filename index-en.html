answers to questions you didn't ask

dasha, so are you a yoga teacher or a manager?

yoga teacher, experience architect, entrepreneur, producer, manager. for 15 years i built projects around wellbeing and sport, and then i turned to my craft and started teaching yoga under my own brand. at some point i realized i don't want to separate the roles: i create experience. sometimes it looks like a yoga class — an immersive practice with live music in an old theater. sometimes like a corporate program or a retreat. the form changes, the essence stays.

what is soft power?

my motto, my philosophy and the name of my brand. soft power is when you change people not through pressure, but through a space where it feels safe for them to change on their own.

what does "experience architect" mean?

it's a person who creates a whole experience for a product or an event — online or offline — so that this experience truly changes the person who meets it. not an event organizer. period. concept, meaning, team, space, visuals, music, promotion and the person's journey inside — all of it is one system.

wow, and we can ask you to do this for our product / brand / company?

yes. a turnkey event or retreat, a corporate wellbeing program, consulting or a strategy session. you can also invite me to talk about your product — we'll find a point of connection if we share the same values.

what are you looking for right now?

international projects at the intersection of wellbeing, longevity, biohacking, ai, body practices and creativity. partnerships with companies and brands for whom people are not a line in a budget. world-class events and spaces. and people i want to build a new world with.
